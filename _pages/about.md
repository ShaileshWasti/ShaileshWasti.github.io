I am a PhD candidate in the [Department of Energy and Mineral Engineering](https://www.eme.psu.edu/) at the [Pennsylvania State University](https://www.psu.edu/), with a minor in [Operations Research](https://www.or.psu.edu/). My dissertation focuses on electricity market design with storage under uncertainty, advised by [Prof. Mort Webster](https://www.eme.psu.edu/directory/mort-d-webster).

Before Penn State, I earned my M.S. in Electrical Engineering (Power & Energy) at the University of Tennessee at Chattanooga, advised by [Prof. Vahid Disfani](https://www.utc.edu/directory/czd631-college-of-engineering-and-computer-science-vahid-rasouli-disfani/czd631), and my B.E. in Electrical Engineering from Tribhuvan University, Kathmandu Engineering College, Nepal.

I have collaborated with PJM Interconnection since 2022 and with ISO New England since 2025, on research supported by ARPA-E (DE-AR0001281) and the U.S. Department of Energy (Award GD0000958), together with PJM and ISO-NE. In summer 2024, I interned at PJM Interconnection under [Anthony Giacomoni](https://www.linkedin.com/in/anthonygiacomoni), Manager of Advanced Analytics, developing long-term electricity demand and load growth scenarios to support infrastructure planning and reliability assessments.

My research interests include electricity markets, optimization, and power systems.
