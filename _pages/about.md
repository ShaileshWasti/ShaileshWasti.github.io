I am a PhD candidate in the [Department of Energy and Mineral Engineering](https://www.eme.psu.edu/) at [Pennsylvania State University](https://www.psu.edu/), pursuing a minor in [Operations Research](https://www.or.psu.edu/). My research focuses on electricity market design with storage under uncertainty, under the supervision of [Prof. Mort Webster](https://www.eme.psu.edu/directory/mort-d-webster).

Prior to my doctoral studies, I earned an M.S. in Electrical Engineering (Power & Energy) from the University of Tennessee at Chattanooga, where I was advised by [Prof. Vahid Disfani](https://www.utc.edu/directory/czd631-college-of-engineering-and-computer-science-vahid-rasouli-disfani/czd631) and worked on a distributed ADMM (Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers) algorithm for energy management in smart grids — the subject of my thesis, [*Distributed Online Algorithms for Energy Management in Smart Grids*](https://scholar.utc.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1845&context=theses). I hold a B.E. in Electrical Engineering from Tribhuvan University, Kathmandu Engineering College, Nepal.

My work bridges academic research and industry application. Since 2022, I have collaborated with PJM Interconnection, and since 2025, with ISO New England. I also completed a summer 2024 internship at PJM Interconnection's Advanced Analytics group, supervised by [Anthony Giacomoni](https://www.linkedin.com/in/anthonygiacomoni).

My interests lie at the intersection of electricity markets, optimization, and power systems, with an emphasis on market mechanisms that support the reliable, economic integration of energy storage and renewable resources.
