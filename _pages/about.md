I am a Ph.D. candidate in the [Department of Energy and Mineral Engineering](https://www.eme.psu.edu/) at [The Pennsylvania State University](https://www.psu.edu/), with a minor in [Operations Research](https://www.or.psu.edu/), advised by [Prof. Mort Webster](https://www.eme.psu.edu/directory/mort-d-webster). My research focuses on electricity market design, energy storage integration, and stochastic optimization for power systems operating under uncertainty.

Prior to Penn State, I earned an M.S. in Electrical Engineering (Power & Energy) from the University of Tennessee at Chattanooga, where I worked with [Prof. Vahid Rasouli Disfani](https://www.utc.edu/directory/czd631-college-of-engineering-and-computer-science-vahid-rasouli-disfani/czd631) on a distributed ADMM (Alternating Direction Method of Multipliers) algorithm for energy management in smart grids — the subject of my thesis, [*Distributed Online Algorithms for Energy Management in Smart Grids*](https://scholar.utc.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1845&context=theses). I hold a B.E. in Electrical Engineering from Tribhuvan University, Kathmandu Engineering College, Nepal.

My research has been conducted in close collaboration with PJM Interconnection since 2022 and ISO New England since 2025, supported by grants from ARPA-E and the U.S. Department of Energy. In Summer 2024, I was a Research Intern at PJM Interconnection, working with [Anthony Giacomoni](https://www.linkedin.com/in/anthonygiacomoni), Manager of Advanced Analytics, on long-term electricity demand and load growth scenarios for infrastructure planning and reliability assessments.

My research interests lie at the intersection of electricity markets, optimization, and power systems, with an emphasis on designing market mechanisms that enable the reliable, economic integration of energy storage and renewable resources under uncertainty.
